Historic Solutions for our Oceans

the power of aquaculture

The quality of our water systems has a tremendous impact on the health of our communities, but global waterways are under threat. Suffering from nutrient pollution due to increasing levels of nitrogen, phosphorous, and C02, deadzones are popping up, suffocating underwater plants and animals, and resulting in unsafe water conditions.

Luckily, there are solutions available to combat nutrition pollution. Heritage aquaculture species offer a wealth of solutions to capture and remove pollution while producing protein-rich foods. In addition to the many environmental benefits, expanding aquaculture farming offers numerous opportunities for green businesses and job creation.

As a large coastal city, New York stands to greatly benefit from establishing better permitting and nitrogen exchange frameworks to support aquaculture farmers and improve the health of our water. After all, in 2021 alone, New York City consumed 979 million gallons of water per day, averaging 116 gallons per person per day.

Hearty bivalves such as oysters, mussels, and clams require minimal input for farmers and offer a nearly carbon-neutral source of animal meat. These bivalves filter nitrogen, phosphates, and numerous other contaminants from our seas. New studies have even demonstrated mussels' ability to filter out microplastics. Additionally, seaweeds capture nitrogen, phosphorous, and C02 while creating oxygen to combat deadzones actively. Moreover, seaweeds are zero-input crops that photosynthesize their own food from sunlight.

It is time to invest in aquaculture farms and farmers as an important step towards building more sustainable food systems, waterways, and businesses.
